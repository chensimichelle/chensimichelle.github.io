UK · Life & Medical Sciences · A-Level Applicant Guide · 2026–27 Entry

University College London

This guide covers 13 undergraduate programmes across UCL's Faculty of Medical Sciences, Faculty of Life Sciences, Faculty of Engineering Sciences, and Faculty of Mathematical & Physical Sciences. Written for students applying with A-levels for 2026–27 entry.

UCL is ranked #9 globally (QS 2026) and is a member of the prestigious Russell Group and G5 universities. With 77,828 undergraduate applications in 2024/25, offer rates range from 34% (Pharmacology) to 84% (Medical Physics), making programme selection critical.

UCAS · Personal statement · Key dates · Common mistakes

How to Apply

UCAS Application Steps

Step-by-step for 2026–27 entry · Most programmes share the 29 January UCAS deadline

May–Jun 2026
Register on UCAS Hub — Create your account at ucas.com. Research all 13 programmes. Check predicted grades before shortlisting.
Jun 2026
Book UCL open days — UCL open days typically fall in June and September. Visit to confirm your programme choice and gather personal statement material.
Sep–Oct 2026
Write personal statement — 80% subject passion, 20% wider skills. One statement covers all 5 UCAS choices — write it for your primary programme.
29 Jan 2027
Submit UCAS application — Equal consideration deadline for most UCL programmes. No late applications considered. Applying before the deadline is always safer.
Feb–May 2027
Receive decisions — UCL does not typically use interviews for these programmes. Decisions are based on academic profile and personal statement.
Aug 2027
A-level results & confirm — Meet your conditional offer. UCL uses UCAS Track to confirm places on results day.

Personal Statement Do's & Don'ts

4,000 characters · One statement for all 5 choices · Subject passion is everything

DO
  • Name specific topics, experiments or readings that excited you
  • Mention books, papers, podcasts or lectures by name
  • Connect work experience or volunteering directly to science
  • Show you understand what the degree involves day-to-day
  • Mention UCL-specific strengths (Francis Crick Institute, Royal Free, UCLH links)
DON'T
  • Open with generic sentences ("I have always loved biology…")
  • List extracurriculars without linking them to science
  • Apply to wildly different programmes across 5 choices — your statement must be coherent
  • Forget the IELTS requirement varies by faculty — check yours early
  • Miss the 29 January deadline — late applications are not considered
